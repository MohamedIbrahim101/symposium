QUIZ

INSTRUCTION AND PROTOCOL

1.Team Formation: Each team can consist of a maximum of four players. Each player must belong to the same college, or organization. Question Types: There may be different types of questions such as multiple-choice questions, true/false questions, short answer questions, fill-in-the-blank questions, audio or video type or a combination of these.
2.Scoring System: Each question may have a different point value. The scoring system should be explained before the start of the competition. The team with the highest score at the end of the competition wins.
3.Timing: Each team may have a limited amount of time to answer each question. This can vary depending on the difficulty of the question. The timing for each question should be announced before the competition starts.
4.Moderator: A moderator will read out the questions and provide any necessary clarifications. The moderator’s decision is final.
5.Use of Electronics: The use of electronic devices such as mobile phones, tablets, or laptops is strictly prohibited. If a team is found using any such device, it may result in disqualification.
6..Disputes: Any disputes should be raised immediately with the moderator. The moderator’s decision is final.
7.Tie-Breaker: In the event of a tie, a tie-breaker round may be held to determine the winner.
8.Conduct: All participants are expected to conduct themselves in a professional and respectful manner. Any inappropriate behavior may result in disqualification.
9.Prizes: Prizes should be announced before the start of the competition. The prize may be in the form of certificates, trophies, or any other suitable reward.
10.Format: The format of the quiz should be clearly communicated to all participants beforehand. It could be a buzzer round or hand raise.
11.Eligibility: Participants must be enrolled in the respective college or organization to take part in the competition.
12.Number of Rounds: The number of rounds should be announced before the start of the quiz. Each round may have a different theme or set of topics based on GK questions.
13.Elimination: In case of a preliminary round followed by a final round, the number of teams that qualify for the final round should be announced beforehand.
14.Penalties: Any team found cheating, using prohibited devices or engaging in inappropriate behavior may be disqualified or penalized.
